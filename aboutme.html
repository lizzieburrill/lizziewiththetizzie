The Diagnosis That Changed Everything

I’m Lizzie, and I found out I was autistic at 38 years old. That was in May 2025, and honestly? I’m still processing what that means for who I am and who I’ve always been. For 38 years, I thought there was something fundamentally wrong with me. I couldn’t understand why everything felt so much harder for me than it seemed for everyone else. Why social situations left me completely drained. Why certain sounds or textures made me want to crawl out of my skin. Why I needed so much more downtime than the people around me just to function. Turns out, I wasn’t broken. I was just autistic.

Life Before Diagnosis

Before my diagnosis, I was the master of masking - I just didn’t know that’s what I was doing. I spent decades studying people like they were a subject I needed to pass exams in. I learned the “right” responses, the appropriate facial expressions, how to make small talk that didn’t feel natural but looked convincing enough. I became so good at performing “normal” that nobody, including me, realized I was performing at all. The cost of that performance? Autistic burnout. The kind where you come home from work and have nothing left - not for your partner, not for your kids, not even for basic self-care. The kind where showering feels like an insurmountable task because every ounce of energy went into maintaining that mask all day. I’ve lived through periods where I was functionally shutdown after work hours, going through the motions but not really present for my own life.

Who I Am Beyond The Diagnosis

I’m a wife and a mum. I work in HR Technology, leading teams that build systems to support people at work - and I’m particularly passionate about making workplaces genuinely inclusive for neurodivergent people. I lead our company’s Ability+ Community and neurodiversity network, which has taken on a whole new meaning since my diagnosis. I love food, travel, and discovering new experiences - though I’m learning that my idea of “discovering” might look different than the typical travel influencer’s version. Give me a detailed itinerary and familiar food options any day over spontaneous adventures.

The Journey Ahead

This website is part of my journey to figure out who Lizzie actually is when she’s not masking, not performing, not trying to be anyone other than herself. It’s about unlearning 38 years of survival strategies and learning how to just… exist authentically. Some days that feels exciting. Some days it feels terrifying. Most days it’s a bit of both. But I’m done spending my energy pretending to be someone I’m not. There’s a freedom in finally understanding why I am the way I am - and in giving myself permission to be exactly that.

Why "Lizzie with the Tizzie"?

Because life can be chaotic, overwhelming, and yes - sometimes I’m in a bit of a tizzy. And that’s okay. That’s just part of being me. This name is me reclaiming language that might have been used to dismiss my very real experiences of overwhelm and turning it into something that’s authentically mine. Welcome to my journey. I’m figuring it out as I go, but at least now I’m figuring out the real me.
