Books

Unmasked by Ellie Middleton

If you’re newly diagnosed or questioning whether you might be autistic or have ADHD, “Unmasked: The Ultimate Guide to Thriving with ADHD and Autism” by Ellie Middleton is essential reading. What makes this book brilliant is how well-organised and accessible it is - perfect for neurodivergent brains that need information presented clearly without overwhelming walls of text. Page after page felt incredibly relatable (so many “oh my god, that’s me” moments), and Middleton strikes the perfect balance between being positive and hopeful while remaining honest and sensitive about real challenges. I especially appreciated that she recognises intersectionality and acknowledges the experiences of those who are further marginalized beyond just being neurodivergent. This book feels like having a knowledgeable, understanding friend walk you through everything you wish someone had told you years ago - it’s the companion I needed when I got my diagnosis at 38.

    Ono Roller

    The Ono Roller is hands down one of the best fidget tools I’ve found for managing sensory needs and anxiety, especially during work meetings or moments when I need to self-regulate without drawing attention. It’s beautifully simple - a smooth, weighted roller that fits perfectly in your hand and glides satisfyingly across your palm, fingers, or even your desk. The tactile feedback is incredibly calming without being distracting, and unlike spinners or clicky fidgets, it’s completely silent, which means I can use it in meetings or calls without annoying everyone around me. The weight gives it a quality feel that cheaper fidgets lack, and there’s something about the repetitive rolling motion that helps ground me when I’m feeling overwhelmed or need to concentrate. It’s small enough to keep in my pocket or bag, durable enough to withstand constant use, and honestly? It’s become as essential as my phone - I genuinely feel a bit lost without it now.
